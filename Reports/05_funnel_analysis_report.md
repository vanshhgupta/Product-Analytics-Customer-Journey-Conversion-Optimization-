# 05. Funnel Analysis Report

## Project
**E-commerce Product Analytics & Growth Optimization**

---

# Objective

The objective of this phase was to analyze the customer purchase journey by evaluating how users progress through the e-commerce funnel—from viewing products to adding them to the cart and ultimately completing a purchase.

This analysis identifies major conversion bottlenecks, evaluates product and category performance, and provides actionable insights to improve customer conversion and revenue generation.

---

# Purchase Funnel Overview

| Funnel Stage | Events |
|--------------|-------:|
| Product Views | **2,664,312** |
| Add to Cart | **69,332** |
| Purchases | **22,457** |

## Key Findings

- The vast majority of customer interactions occur during the product viewing stage.
- Only a small fraction of product views lead to cart additions.
- An even smaller proportion results in completed purchases.

### Business Insight

The purchase funnel experiences significant customer drop-off between the browsing and purchase stages, indicating opportunities to improve product discovery, customer engagement, and checkout experience.

---

# Funnel Conversion Analysis

| Metric | Value |
|--------|------:|
| View → Cart Conversion | **2.60%** |
| Cart → Purchase Conversion | **32.39%** |
| Overall View → Purchase Conversion | **0.84%** |

## Key Findings

- Approximately **2.6%** of product views result in an item being added to the cart.
- Nearly one-third of customers who add products to their cart complete a purchase.
- The overall purchase conversion rate remains below **1%**, highlighting significant opportunity for optimization.

### Business Insight

The largest opportunity lies in increasing the percentage of visitors who progress from product viewing to purchase intent.

---

# Cart Abandonment Analysis

| Metric | Value |
|--------|------:|
| Cart Additions | **69,332** |
| Purchases | **22,457** |
| Abandoned Carts | **46,875** |
| Cart Abandonment Rate | **67.61%** |

## Key Findings

- More than two-thirds of shopping carts do not result in completed purchases.
- Only about one-third of cart additions successfully convert into transactions.

### Business Recommendations

Possible reasons for cart abandonment include:

- High product prices
- Unexpected shipping costs
- Lengthy checkout process
- Limited payment options
- Stock availability issues

Reducing cart abandonment represents one of the highest-impact opportunities for increasing revenue.

# Overall Business Insights

The funnel analysis highlights several important patterns:

- Customer engagement is heavily concentrated at the product browsing stage.
- Only **2.60%** of viewed products are added to the cart.
- More than **67%** of shopping carts are abandoned before checkout.
- Several products achieve exceptionally high conversion rates, demonstrating strong customer demand.
- Many popular products receive thousands of views but fail to generate purchases, representing high-priority optimization opportunities.
- Certain product categories consistently outperform others and should receive greater merchandising focus.

---

# Recommendations

Based on the funnel analysis, the following actions are recommended:

- Improve product pages for high-traffic, low-conversion products.
- Simplify the checkout process to reduce cart abandonment.
- Promote high-converting products and categories through personalized recommendations.
- Introduce cart reminder notifications and promotional incentives.
- Analyze pricing, inventory availability, and product content for underperforming products.

---

# Conclusion

The funnel analysis provides a detailed understanding of customer progression through the purchasing journey.

The findings reveal that while the platform successfully attracts customer attention, substantial drop-off occurs before purchase completion. Addressing these bottlenecks can significantly improve conversion rates and overall business performance.
