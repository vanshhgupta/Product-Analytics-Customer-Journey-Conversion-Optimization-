# 06. Product Metrics Report

## Project
**E-commerce Product Analytics & Growth Optimization**

---

# Objective

The objective of this phase was to evaluate product performance using customer engagement, purchasing behavior, and interaction metrics. The analysis identifies the products that generate the highest attention, strongest purchase intent, repeat purchases, and overall engagement.

This provides valuable insights for merchandising, inventory planning, marketing campaigns, and product optimization.

---

# Executive Summary

| KPI | Value |
|------|------:|
| Total Products | **235,061** |
| Products Viewed | **234,838** |
| Products Added to Cart | **23,903** |
| Products Purchased | **12,025** |
| Total Customers | **1,407,580** |
| Total Product Interactions | **2,756,101** |

### Key Findings

- Nearly every product in the catalog received at least one customer view.
- Only **23,903 products (10.2%)** were ever added to a shopping cart.
- Only **12,025 products (5.1%)** generated at least one purchase.
- Product interaction is highly concentrated around a relatively small subset of the catalog.

# Product Engagement Score

A weighted engagement score was calculated using:

- View = 1 point
- Add to Cart = 3 points
- Purchase = 5 points

Highest scoring products include:

| Product ID | Engagement Score |
|------------|----------------:|
| 461686 | **4,122** |
| 187946 | **3,416** |
| 5411 | **2,352** |
| 219512 | **1,944** |
| 257040 | **1,933** |

### Key Findings

- Product **461686** dominates overall engagement by performing consistently across views, cart additions, and purchases.

### Business Insight

Products with high engagement but comparatively lower purchases should be reviewed for pricing, product presentation, customer reviews, or inventory availability.

# Product Interaction Distribution

| Event Type | Unique Products | Total Interactions | Percentage |
|------------|----------------:|------------------:|-----------:|
| View | **234,838** | **2,664,312** | **96.67%** |
| Add to Cart | **23,903** | **69,332** | **2.52%** |

### Key Findings

- Nearly all product interactions consist of browsing activity.
- Only a small percentage of products progress to cart additions and completed purchases.
- Customer engagement declines sharply after the product discovery stage.

---

# Overall Business Insights

The product metrics analysis reveals several important patterns:

- Customer attention is concentrated on a relatively small number of products.
- Product **461686** consistently outperforms across views, cart additions, purchases, and engagement score.
- High repeat purchase rates indicate strong customer loyalty for selected products.
- Several highly viewed products receive limited purchases, suggesting opportunities for conversion optimization.
- Product engagement remains heavily skewed toward browsing activity, reinforcing findings from the funnel analysis.

---

# Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following actions are recommended:

- Increase visibility of high-converting and high-performing products.
- Optimize product pages for high-view, low-purchase products.
- Launch loyalty campaigns for products with strong repeat purchase behavior.
- Prioritize inventory availability for best-selling products.
- Use engagement scores to identify products suitable for featured promotions and recommendation engines.

---

# Conclusion

The product metrics analysis provides a comprehensive evaluation of product performance across customer engagement and purchasing behavior.

The findings highlight both top-performing products and products with untapped potential, enabling data-driven decisions in merchandising, marketing, and inventory management.
